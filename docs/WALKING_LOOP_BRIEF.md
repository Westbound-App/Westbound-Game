# WESTBOUND — Walking Loop Generation Brief

The three clips that turn the live feed from a photograph into a window.
Generate these in your video tool (Sora in ChatGPT, or Veo in the Gemini
app — the same accounts that made the reference photos). The site's
seamless looper is already built and waiting; drop the .mp4 files into an
`Incoming-scenes` folder and push, exactly like the photo batches.

## Before every generation

Attach these reference images (from `docs/references/`):

1. `together/canon-together-summer-walk-01.png` — the identity anchor
2. One walker angle + one Beacon angle that match the shot
3. Then paste the character blocks from `docs/CHARACTER_BIBLE.md`

## Clip 1 — "The Walk" (the hero loop)

> Continuous 8-second tracking shot from behind, camera smoothly following
> at slow walking pace, about ten feet back and slightly above. The same
> man and dog as the reference images walking a two-lane rural road
> through Maine pine forest, summer afternoon. He walks with an easy,
> unhurried stride; the dog walks freely beside him, no leash, tail
> relaxed and swinging. Steady gimbal documentary feel, constant pace,
> no cuts, no zooms, no camera shake, nothing entering or leaving frame.
> Photoreal, 35mm documentary grade, muted true-to-life colors.

**Loop notes (important):** constant walking pace the whole clip, no
one-off events (no birds flying through, no cars), similar framing at the
first and last second — the site crossfades the end into the beginning,
and uniform clips loop invisibly.

## Clip 2 — "The Connection" (the bond beat)

> Same characters, road, and camera as the reference images. Over 8
> seconds: the dog trots two steps ahead, glances back up at the man, and
> the man gives a small smile and nod as the dog falls back in beside
> him. Both keep walking the entire time, camera following steadily from
> behind and slightly to the side. No leash, no cuts, no camera shake,
> photoreal documentary grade.

This is the clip that answers "do they feel real together" — generate
extra takes and keep the one where the glance feels natural.

## Clip 3 — "Golden Hour" (same as Clip 1, different light)

Same prompt as Clip 1, replacing the light description with:

> late golden hour, low warm sun ahead of them, long soft shadows
> stretching toward the camera, gentle haze.

## QC before pushing (reject a take if any fail)

- [ ] Feet touch the ground — no floating or sliding gait
- [ ] Beacon's markings stable the whole clip (white chest, paws, tail
      tip; no leash appearing mid-clip)
- [ ] The pack, cap, and clothing match the references throughout
- [ ] Pace stays constant; nothing dramatic enters the frame
- [ ] It loops in your head: imagine the last frame jumping to the first

## Delivery

16:9, highest resolution offered, .mp4. Name them anything; put them in
`Incoming-scenes/`, push, and say the word — wiring them into the live
feed (including the vertical crop for mobile) is a fifteen-minute job on
